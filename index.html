ALTERNATIVES

The Tucson area is also home to many incredible Airbnbs and resorts. We recommend not waiting until the last minute. October in Southern Arizona is peak season – places will book up fast.

While in Tucson
Come a little early, stay a little late, or squeeze in some sightseeing during the days. We recommend renting a car to get around – Uber is always an option, but Tucson does not have reliable public transportation.

DINING

Tito & Pep

‘Tucsonan’ cuisine – a lot of local Sonoran ingredients, a great wine list. Not the most picturesque location, but our favorite meal in Tucson these days.

Agustin Kitchen

Classic menu with a lot of local ingredients. Located in the Mercado San Agustin – a small outdoor market, even nice to sit and have a cocktail.

Feast

Another one of our favorites – an unexpected menu from Doug Levy, a culinary genius (and the caterer of the wedding).

Maynards

Inside a historic train depot in downtown Tucson, our special occasion spot. Afterwards, stroll across the street to the Congress Hotel (where John Dillinger was captured in 1934) for a drink in the Tap Room or the historic lobby.

Anello

Incredible pizza, small, not to be missed vegetable plates, and a natural wine selection. A little gem of a place, special for Tucson.

Five Points

A delicious breakfast and lunch spot with a great small market for local produce, natural wine, etc.

The Little One

Breakfast / lunch on weekdays only. Located in the middle of downtown, it caters to the office lunch scene, but offers up some really delicious Tucson-style Mexican.

El Charro

A classic Tucson spot. They claim to be the first Mexican restaurant in the US – and the home of the chimichanga.

The Coronet

Nice stop off for an afternoon snack and glass of wine or a light lunch. On the edge of the barrio – take in a stroll afterwards.

Taco Apsom

Meat-centric tacos in South Tucson.

Aqui Con El Nene

Another great taco option and a perfect opportunity to try a Sonoran Dog.

Seis Tacos

Johan's favorite Tucson tacos.

Exo Coffee

Good cup of coffee.

Presta Coffee

More good coffee. Stop off at their Mercado location and grab a Mexican pastry from La Estrella next door (their mini donuts are delicious).

ACTIVITIES

Saguaro National Park

The park is in two parts, one on the west side, one east of the city. The west is more picturesque. Drive up through Gates Pass (amazing sunset view of the valley) and then on to the Bajada Loop, a good place to see a lot of prime Saguaro – you can drive through and pull off for a few mini hikes.

The Desert Museum

A zoo, natural history museum, aquarium, and botanical garden all in one. It’s TripAdvisor’s #1 activity for a reason...

Mt Lemmon Highway

Drive up Mt. Lemmon to Windy Point Vista and watch the sunset. You go from desert to pines in under an hour – it’s the equivalent of driving from the deserts of Mexico north to Canada. Very, very scenic.

Mission San Xavier del Bac

The ‘white dove of the desert’ – a Catholic mission from the 1700s. 10 miles south of downtown on the Tohono O'odham Nation. Often there is a delicious fry bread stall out front.

The Presidio District

Good for a stroll, see some historic Tucson architecture. Make a stop at the Tucson Museum of Art, do some shopping at Old Town Artisans – records, pottery, jewelry, and other eclectic finds – and grab a bite at El Charro.

Catalina State Park

Beautiful state park 25 minutes north of downtown Tucson. Miles of hiking trails at the base of the Catalina Mountains.

Tohono Chul

Really special botanic garden. Focused on the Sonoran Desert.

The Center for Creative Photography

Started by Ansel Adams and the University of Arizona, this museum is home to over 270 complete archives – Adams, Edward Weston, Lola Álvarez Bravo, Edward Weston, Harry Callahan, W. Eugene Smith, and Garry Winogrand. Check the site for upcoming exhibitions.

The Pima Air and Space Museum

One of the world's largest non-government funded aerospace museums with 400 aircraft spread out over 80 acres. Try to land a tour of the boneyard, the largest in the world, at Davis-Monthan Air Force Base.

SHOPPING

Time Market

Best little grocery store – amazing wine selection, great cheeses, snacks, produce, beautiful fresh flowers. And also good pizza / sandwiches to go.

Monsoon Chocolate

The most incredible bonbons you’ll ever have.

Desert Vintage

Most beautiful vintage store out there.

Stewart Boots

The best, classic cowboy boots out there. If you have 3-4 hours to kill, have Victor measure your feet for the perfect custom pair – he’s one of the last standing boot experts out there and retiring this year.

Mercado San Agustin + the Annex

Pretty market with a few stores, a lot of local finds, food options, and a farmer’s market on Thursdays

The Rillito Park Sunday Heirloom Farmers Market

Local produce, spices, breakfast burritos, fresh coffee, tamales, and so much more.

Book Stop

Great spot for weird art books and maps.

Old Paint Records

Johan’s favorite Tucson record store. An incredible selection of jazz and world records at reasonable prices.

SIDE TRIPS

Tombstone

A little over an hour outside of Tucson – a very touristy spot, but for a reason. Saddle up to the bar at Big Nose Kate’s Saloon. Visit the world’s largest rose bush. Take in a live reenactment of the gunfight at O.K. Corral.

Bisbee

A little less than half an hour past Tombstone sits this historic copper mining, mountain town. Plenty of haunted spots – stay at The Copper Queen Hotel if you’re looking for something a little spooky.

Puerto Penasco

Head south of the border, in under four hours you can be beachside in Mexico, in the northern shores of the Sea of Cortez. Eat amazing seafood and explore some incredible tide pools.

Arcosanti

Founded by Italian architect, philosopher Paolo Soleri and started as an experimental community. Book tickets for a tour of the grounds and pick up a wind chime while you’re there.

Aravaipa

Stay at Aravaipa Farms in one of the most beautiful canyons you’ll ever see. This cozy retreat features an organic orchard and gourmet cuisine. Be sure to reserve a pass to hike the canyon – and bring water shoes.
